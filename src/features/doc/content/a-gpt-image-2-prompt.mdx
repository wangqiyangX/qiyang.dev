What stands out to me is how the prompt controls the image without describing
every pixel. It starts from a familiar camera setup, then layers in character
design, lighting, texture, and platform-specific aesthetics. The mirror selfie
and flash reflection make the scene feel like a real captured moment, while the
anime-inspired features keep the image from becoming a plain cosplay photo.

The useful structure is:

- **Camera setup**: phone, flash, mirror selfie, visible reflection.
- **Scene texture**: slightly messy room, casual light, warm shadows.
- **Character direction**: anime-inspired faces, close grouping, intimate poses.
- **Style boundary**: semi-realistic, grainy, cinematic, TikTok-like.
- **Final constraint**: real people in cosplay, not fully animated characters.

That last constraint is doing a lot of work. It tells the model where the image
should sit on the realism spectrum, which is often the difference between a
generic anime composition and something that feels like a stylized real-world
photo. I am saving it here as a small reference for future image-generation
experiments.
